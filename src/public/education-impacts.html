Extreme Natural Disasters

With increasing global temperatures at a steady rate of 1.5 degrees celcius, the earth has experienced higher intensity earthquakes, larger tsunamis and increased drought. In just February 2020 alone, 26 countries all experienced natural disasters which lead to many lives lost.

Sea Levels Rising

Global mean sea level has risen about 8–9 inches (21–24 centimeters) since 1880, with about a third of that coming in just the last two and a half decades. The rising water level is mostly due to a combination of melt water from glaciers and ice sheets and thermal expansion of seawater as it warms. In 2020, global mean sea level was 91.3 millimeters (3.6 inches) above the 1993 average, making it the highest annual average in the satellite record (1993-present).

Mass Deforestation

When more trees are cut down, there will be less trees to absorb the carbon dioxide from the atmospheric air. Thus, more greenhouse gases in the air trapping heat and leading to global warming.

Fun Facts

The Hottest Year

Earth’s global average surface temperature in 2020 tied with 2016 as the warmest year on record, according to an analysis by NASA.

Mass Deforestation

Mass deforestation contributes the most to the greenhouse effect as trees provide almost 67% of our oxygen and releases the most Carbon dioxide gas in return.

Icebergs

Every year the earth loses 1.2 trillion tons of ice. The rate of ice melting has nearly doubled in speed over the past 20 years causing sea-levels to rise significantly.

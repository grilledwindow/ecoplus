Reduce, Reuse, Recycle

The three R's all help to cut down on the amount of waste we throw away. The best way to manage waste is to not produce it. E.g. We can reduce the usage of plastic by bringing our own bag during grocery trips. Reusing products means less fossil fuels will be burned to create new products. Lastly, recyling also reduces emissions of greenhouse gases, such as carbon dioxide, methane, nitrous oxide and chlorofluorocarbons, that contribute to global climate change.

Sustainable Transportation

Sustainable Transportation refers to traveling without emitting large amounts of greenhouse gases such as CO2 and Methane. For example, instead of using Fuel Vehicles we can use electric vehicles, walk or cycle. For example, the train system in Sweden is extemely energy-efficient as the trains are built using 98% recyclable materials.

Renewable Energy

Renewable energy helps combat climate change as it creates no direct greenhouse emissions. It can also decrease pollution as wind, solar, hydro-electric systems produce no air pollution which decreases the threat to our health. Lastly, it is a reliable source of power. The energy is converted to electrical energy and can power our appliances as well as cities for it to function.
